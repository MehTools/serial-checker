What is a Solid Serial Number? The Holy Grail of Fancy Notes

📅 March 2026⏱ 5 min read

Of all fancy serial number patterns, the solid serial number is the holy grail. Every single digit is identical — a sight so striking it stops people in their tracks.

The nine possible solids: 11111111, 22222222, 33333333, 44444444, 55555555, 66666666, 77777777, 88888888, and 99999999. That is nine notes out of roughly 100 million possible serials per denomination.

Why Are Solid Notes So Valuable?

Three factors drive solid note value: extreme rarity, instant visual impact, and international collector demand. The number 8 is considered auspicious in Chinese culture, making 88888888 notes sought after by collectors globally — cross-market demand that regularly pushes prices above any other solid serial.

Condition Matters Enormously

An uncirculated solid note can sell for 3 to 5 times a worn circulated example of the same serial. If you find one, handle it by the edges only and sleeve it immediately. For notes worth over $500, PMG grading is almost always worth the cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a solid serial number?
A solid serial number has every digit identical — for example 11111111, 55555555, or 88888888 on a US dollar bill.
How much is a solid serial number worth?
A solid $1 bill typically fetches $500 to $2,000. A solid $100 note has sold for over $10,000 in exceptional condition.
How rare is a solid serial number?
There are only 9 possible solid serials per denomination. The odds of receiving one in circulation are roughly 1 in 11 million.
Which solid number is most valuable?
88888888 is generally the most valuable due to the significance of 8 in Chinese culture, creating strong international collector demand.
