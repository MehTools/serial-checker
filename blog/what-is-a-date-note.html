What is a Date Note? Birthday Banknotes Explained

📅 March 2026⏱ 5 min read

Date notes are banknotes whose serial numbers spell a recognisable calendar date. The combination of personalisation and monetary value makes them one of the fastest-growing collector categories — and a genuinely unique gift.

Example: A $1 bill with serial 07041776 represents July 4, 1776 — American Independence Day. Serial 25121990 represents December 25, 1990 — a Christmas birthday.

Why Date Notes Make Perfect Gifts

A birthday note is arguably the most thoughtful money-as-gift option available. Instead of a generic banknote in a card, a crisp dollar bill whose serial matches someone's exact date of birth is a memorable keepsake that is also legal tender. The market for birthday notes has grown substantially, driven by social media and the r/birthdaynotes community on Reddit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a date note?
A date note has a serial number that can be read as a calendar date. For example 12251995 represents December 25, 1995 in MMDDYYYY format.
Are date notes worth money?
Date notes matching significant dates sell for $15 to $75 above face value. Notes matching the recipient's birth date make highly personalised gifts.
How do I find a banknote with my birthday?
Search eBay for your birthday in MMDDYYYY format. Our free checker also detects whether any note you have spells a date in all three recognised formats.
What date formats work?
MMDDYYYY (US format), DDMMYYYY (European) and YYYYMMDD (ISO). A note may qualify under more than one format.
